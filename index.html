Innovations

Germ Free Airports


• Germ free airports (GFA) is a business proposal by Solid Connections, an upcoming company that deals with a wide range of businesses based in Zambian.


• GFA is meant to be used at airports to clean trays used to put belongings when passing through the security scan.

Problems

The health and bacteria in airports; specifically the trays used to scan our hand luggage. They carry all sorts of pathogens (due to the large number of people from all parts of the world with exposure to different germs) without having them cleaned.

Target Market

Our target market is the world’s busiest airports/ largest airports by passenger number (international airports). Our target market is based on the fact that these airports have a lot more people traveling from all over to different destinations; which means a more health risks.

Solution

Our solution is to design machines for all airports that will automatically kill pathogens on trays after each person’s use leaving them clean. This will be done using a quick drying disinfectant and or Ultraviolet irradiation light that kills all types of pathogens.

Competition

According to our research only one competitor has tried out the idea using mineral nano-crystals that react with light producing a chemical oxidation that is said to be stronger than bleach. This was tried out at Ohio’s Akron airport in partnership with a local hospital.
